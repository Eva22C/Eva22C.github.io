CAO Ziyan

Due to the similarities between Full pinyin and Shuangpin input methods, here are some findings based on research and practical experience to compare them:

  • Input speed: Although Shuangpin may require additional keystrokes to enter each character, it can still achieve high input speed with practice. However, for users who are already proficient with Full Pinyin, they may find it difficult to switch to Shuangpin with considerable speed. In my experience, Shuangpin is more effective for certain types of characters, such as those that require two or more keystrokes to fully spell, so the speed difference between the two methods may also depend on the type and frequency of the characters entered.
  • Use of keyboard: In Shuangpin, each key corresponds to a fixed letter or tone, so users can place their fingers on the main line and type more efficiently without looking at the keyboard. This also means that all letters and tones are equally important, unlike in Full Pinyin, where some letters are rarely used and may be harder to remember. Based on keystroke frequency analysis, depending on my experience, Shuangpin may actually be more effective than Full Pinyin, although it requires more keystrokes per character.
  • Learning curve: Based on my own experiences and those of friends in Hong Kong who have not studied Pinyin, we found that beginners of Chinese pinyin may find Shuangpin easier to learn because the correspondence between letters and tones is more intuitive and consistent. On the other hand, a complete spelling method requires users to remember the spelling of each character individually, which can be daunting for some Chinese pinyin beginners. However, a complete spelling approach may provide more opportunities to practice and reinforce the correct pronunciation of each character, since the user must type each tone separately.
  • Error rate: Shuangpin has been shown to have a lower error rate than Full Pinyin, because letter combination rules help users avoid common spelling mistakes. For example, in Shuangpin, "qi" and "ji" are typed using different letter combinations, which reduces the possibility of misspelling one letter with the other. However, Shuangpin does not support entering characters with multiple pronunciations, which can lead to errors if the user chooses the wrong pronunciations based on context.
  • User satisfaction: User satisfaction with an input method depends on a number of factors, such as input habits, personal preferences, and specific usage scenarios. In my experience, as a user who likes fast typing and has experience with QWERTY typing, I tend to choose Full Pinyin, while those who value ease of learning and accuracy tend to choose Shuangpin.

LIN Zerong

The experience of Cangjie input method and fast input method: These two input methods use strokes to input the characters we need, but compared with the Pinyin input method, they are both far behind. We have summarized the following points:

  1. Cangjie and fast input methods have a small audience and slow development. The earliest Cangjie was born in Taiwan, but now only Hong Kong and Macau are using it, and Taiwan is gradually turning to the Pinyin input method. However, due to the lack of Pinyin in Mandarin, Hong Kong people are forced to use Cangjie. The pinyin input method can type out phrases, make associations, and record users' habits to generate keywords, but the Cangjie input method is stuck in place and does not make progress.
  2. It is very difficult for Cangjie to decompose characters and codes. In addition, the complexity of traditional characters has resulted in many characters that cannot be separated and input. Such as “冎” need to be “月月”, it is difficult to understand.
  3. Compared with Wubi, the keyboards of Cangjie and fast input methods are less user-friendly. Wubi’s keyboards are designed according to typing speed and comfort, but Cangjie’s "日月金木水火土" correspond to "ABCDEFG" respectively. kind of like a gimmick or something.

Of course, Cangjie and Express input methods are also very good input methods after being proficient, but with the change of people's daily usage habits, Cangjie input method has gradually become less popular.

Due to the development of modern computers, Pinyin has been used extensively, while Chinese characters have gradually been forgotten. The emergence of the Wubi input method is a kind of rescue of the cultural inheritance of Chinese characters today. The use of the Wubi input method can reduce the problem of forgetting characters when picking up a pen.
